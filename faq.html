Frequently Asked Questions

What is Adventure Peak?

Adventure Peak is a fun, moderately competitive summer camp located in the Adirondack Mountains. We aim to provide 8-17 year olds with fun and engaging activities, while also providing leadership opportunities, social and emotional learning, survival skills, and most importantly, lifelong memories!

Is Adventure Peak a sleepaway camp?

Yes, Adventure Peak is a sleepaway camp. We ask that you please keep your child at camp for the entire duration of the program that they were signed up for.

How long will my child be away at camp?

Depending on which program your child is signed up for, your child will be at camp for either 6 weeks, 8 weeks, or 12 weeks. We have a full, fleshed-out curriculum for every part of the day, hence why we strongly ask that your child remain enrolled in camp for the entire time.

What if my child needs to leave camp before the end of program?

Because of our heavy enrollment numbers, we ask that you only submit an enrollment form if you know your child will be available for the entire program, or consider enrolling in our shorter programs.. We generally do not accommodate requests for early dismissal from camp. In the event of an emergency, please contact our office at (xxx)xxx-xxxx

What if my child gets homesick?

Surprisingly enough, we've found that children generally do not feel overwhelmingly homesick. While the occasional homesickness is fairly common, it is a completely normal and natural feeling. Our camp counselors are highly trained in child care and will connect with your child to ensure they feel at home. If you're worried about your child and want to stay in touch, we highly encourage sending letters to our very special Parent Inbox, located at our facility. We've found that staying in contact with your child has boosted morale and makes campers feel more at-ease knowing that their families are thinking about them!

What is your policy on technology?

Cell phones, laptops, and any device capable of communication is strictly prohibited. We understand that campers may want to stay in touch with family, and we have designated times throughout the week to write letters back home. We also do not permit cameras or other devices capable of taking pictures or videos to protect your child, as well as others. We do allow MP3 players and headphones, so long as they do not violate the policies above. Any devices that violate the technology agreement will be confiscated and will be returned on the last day of camp.

What should my child bring?

We ask that parents provide their child with enough clothing to last for a week. Camp shirts and sweaters are covered with your child's tuition, but we still ask that you bring a few articles of clothing so that your child has a choice in what to wear. We do not provide toothbrushes, so please make sure to have your child bring one. If your child has any special items, such as toys, journals, books, etc., we highly encourage bringing that so that your child can practice their hobbies during free time.

What if my child forgets something?

Our camp shop will have anythign that your child may need should there be an accident, such as toothbrushes, toothpaste, soap, shampoo, conditioner, as well as souvenirs. Since we have a good balance between boys and girls, if your child is a girl, she will NOT be expected to purchase feminine products.

You mentioned that Adventure Peak has a camp shop. Should my child bring money?

Older campers are set up with a "Camper Account" so that they can make purchases as needed. You can reload their balance straight from home if you see that your child is low on funds. We do this to avoid physical cash being lost or stolen on the premesis. We also do not take credit or debit cards.
